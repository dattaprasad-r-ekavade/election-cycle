# CAMPAIGN MODE: 10 Scenarios

## Overview

Campaign Mode offers 10 hand-crafted scenarios with unique backstories, opponents, and narrative arcs. Unlike Quick Play (procedural), these are designed experiences with scripted moments.

**Unlock Structure:**
- Scenarios 1-3: Unlocked from start
- Scenarios 4-6: Unlock after completing any 2 scenarios
- Scenarios 7-9: Unlock after completing any 5 scenarios
- Scenario 10: Unlock after completing all 9 previous scenarios

**Difficulty Curve:**
- Scenarios 1-3: Tutorial-adjacent, forgiving
- Scenarios 4-6: Standard difficulty
- Scenarios 7-9: Challenging, requires mastery
- Scenario 10: "True ending" difficulty

## LAUNCH SCENARIOS (1-5)

---

### SCENARIO 1: "First Timer"
**Difficulty:** Easy | **Best for:** New players

#### Backstory
You're a complete political nobody—a local accountant who got fed up watching the town council ignore potholes for three years. You filed the paperwork as a joke. Now you're on the ballot.

#### District: Millbrook
- **Type:** Small suburban town
- **Population:** 12,000
- **Vibe:** Sleepy, aging, suspicious of change
- **Crisis:** The town's only grocery store is closing

#### Opponent: MARGARET HOLLOWAY
- **Age:** 67
- **Background:** Three-term incumbent, former school principal
- **Personality:** Dismissive, condescending, underestimates you completely
- **Weakness:** She's been coasting; hasn't actually done anything in years
- **Attack style:** Treats you like a child having a tantrum

#### Unique Events
- **Day 2:** Margaret publicly calls you "adorable" for trying
- **Day 4:** A local reporter actually takes you seriously (first endorsement opportunity)
- **Day 6 (Debate):** Margaret doesn't prepare—she's beatable if you've done the work

#### Scripted Moments
- If you win: Margaret's concession speech is genuinely shocked
- If you lose but get >45%: "Closer than expected—not bad for a first timer"

#### Theme
*"Everyone starts somewhere."*

---

### SCENARIO 2: "The Family Business"
**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium | **Best for:** Learning Kapital mechanics

#### Backstory
Your family has run this district for three generations. Grandfather was mayor. Mother was on the council. Now it's your turn. There's just one problem: you're the disappointing one. The one who moved away. The one who "didn't care about the legacy." Now you're back, and everyone's watching to see if you'll live up to the name—or finally prove them right about you.

#### District: Carver Falls
- **Type:** Old money river town
- **Population:** 28,000
- **Vibe:** Everyone knows everyone's business, especially yours
- **Crisis:** The old textile mill (your family built it) is being sold to developers

#### Opponent: RAYMOND CROSS
- **Age:** 52
- **Background:** Self-made businessman, moved here 10 years ago
- **Personality:** Charming outsider, positions himself as "fresh blood"
- **Weakness:** He's hiding bankruptcy in his past
- **Attack style:** "This district deserves someone who CHOSE to be here"

#### Unique Events
- **Day 1:** Your mother gives you "the talk" about not embarrassing the family
- **Day 3:** Old family donors expect favors—taking their money has strings
- **Day 5:** A cousin offers dirt on Raymond... if you're willing to use it

#### Scripted Moments
- Family legacy affects starting Legitimacy (+2) and Influence (+1)
- Certain NPCs remember your grandfather (positive or negative)
- If you take too much donor money: "You're just like your uncle" (family scandal)

#### Theme
*"Legacy is a gift and a cage."*

### SCENARIO 3: "The Transplant"
**Difficulty:** Medium | **Best for:** Learning Influence/trust mechanics

#### Backstory
You moved here five years ago. Opened a small business. Joined the community board. Now you're running for office, and the locals have one question: "Who the hell do you think you are?"

You're an outsider. You'll always be an outsider. The question is whether that's a weakness... or your greatest strength.

#### District: Havenport
- **Type:** Coastal working-class town
- **Population:** 18,000
- **Vibe:** Insular, proud, "we take care of our own"
- **Crisis:** Fish processing plant announcing layoffs (again)

#### Opponent: BOBBY "HUTCH" HUTCHINS
- **Age:** 44
- **Background:** Born here, works at the docks, everyone's drinking buddy
- **Personality:** Salt of the earth, genuine, not very smart
- **Weakness:** He's a nice guy, not a leader—people like him, don't respect him
- **Attack style:** "I've got nothing against [you], but this is OUR town"

#### Unique Events
- **Day 2:** A local asks where you were during the '18 storm (you weren't here)
- **Day 4:** Someone from your old city shows up—helps or hurts depending on handling
- **Day 5:** Hutch's campaign does "locals only" rally—how do you respond?

#### Scripted Moments
- Starting trust with all NPCs: -15 (outsider penalty)
- Unique dialogue options about being an outsider
- Can flip the narrative: "Fresh eyes see what locals miss"

#### Theme
*"Home is where they let you stay."*

### SCENARIO 4: "The Scandal"
**Difficulty:** Medium-Hard | **Best for:** Learning scandal/damage control

#### Backstory
Six months ago, you were the frontrunner. Beloved. Unstoppable. Then the photos leaked. They weren't illegal. They weren't even that bad. But they were embarrassing, and this district doesn't forget.

Now you're the "scandal candidate." Every handshake comes with a smirk. Every interview starts with "So, about those photos..." You could drop out. Everyone expects you to. But you didn't get this far by meeting expectations.

#### District: Greendale
- **Type:** Affluent suburbs
- **Population:** 45,000
- **Vibe:** Performatively moral, secretly messy
- **Crisis:** School board corruption scandal (ironic, given your situation)

#### Opponent: PATRICIA MANNING
- **Age:** 55
- **Background:** Church organizer, "concerned mother," professional pearl-clutcher
- **Personality:** Sanctimonious, fake-nice, lives for moral outrage
- **Weakness:** Her son got a DUI she covered up
- **Attack style:** "I pray for my opponent's... situation."

#### Scripted Scandal
- **"The Photos":** -20 Legitimacy, -15 starting trust with all NPCs
- Cannot be removed—must be managed
- Scandal comes up in EVERY debate round

#### Unique Events
- **Day 2:** Supporter from before the scandal publicly defends you (or doesn't)
- **Day 4:** A reporter offers to bury the story... for a price
- **Day 5:** Patricia's son's DUI surfaces—do you use it? (MAJOR choice)

#### Scripted Moments
- Unique "scandal response" dialogue options throughout
- Can lean INTO the scandal: "Yes, I'm human. What's her excuse?"
- If you win: Redemption arc ending
- If you weaponize Patricia's son: Pyrrhic victory ending

#### Theme
*"Everyone's got photos. Some people's just got leaked."*

### SCENARIO 5: "The Rematch"
**Difficulty:** Hard | **Best for:** Experienced players

#### Backstory
Four years ago, you ran for this seat. You lost by 127 votes. One hundred and twenty-seven. You've spent every day since then thinking about what you'd do differently.

Your opponent? Same guy. He's been a mediocre councilman for four years. Nothing's changed. Nothing's gotten better. But people don't like admitting they were wrong.

This time, you're not the underdog. You're the grudge.

#### District: Westfield
- **Type:** Mixed suburban/urban
- **Population:** 52,000
- **Vibe:** Divided, cynical, low voter turnout
- **Crisis:** Housing costs have doubled since the last election

#### Opponent: COUNCILMAN DEREK WALSH
- **Age:** 48
- **Background:** Career politician, smooth talker, nothing guy
- **Personality:** Slippery, never commits, master of non-answers
- **Weakness:** His record is public—four years of nothing
- **Attack style:** "My opponent is stuck in the past. I'm focused on the future."

#### Unique Mechanics
- **"Last Time" memories:** NPCs reference your previous campaign
- Some remember you positively ("You deserved to win")
- Some remember you negatively ("You were too aggressive")
- **127 Votes:** Counter appears in UI—you need to flip 127 people

#### Unique Events
- **Day 1:** A volunteer from last time shows up—they never stopped believing
- **Day 3:** Someone who voted against you last time wants to talk
- **Day 6 (Debate):** Walsh tries the same attacks that worked before

#### Scripted Moments
- If you win by exactly 127 votes: Special "poetic justice" ending
- If you lose again: Different ending based on margin (worse or better than last time)
- Can confront Walsh about specific broken promises from 4 years ago

#### Theme
*"Revenge is a dish best served at the ballot box."*

### SCENARIO 6: "The Machine"
**Difficulty:** Hard

#### Concept
You're running against the party machine. They own the local newspaper. They own the unions. They own the cops. Everyone tells you to drop out "for your own good."

A story about fighting institutional power with nothing but conviction.

#### Key Elements
- Opponent is backed by shadowy "party committee"
- Constant pressure/threats from machine operatives
- Can try to work within the system or burn it down
- Unique "corruption" meter—the machine tries to buy you

---

### SCENARIO 7: "The Proxy War"
**Difficulty:** Hard

#### Concept
You and your opponent don't matter. You're both puppets. The real fight is between two billionaires using your local election as a test case for their national ambitions.

A story about finding your own voice when powerful people are speaking for you.

#### Key Elements
- Both candidates have "handlers" who tell them what to say
- Can follow the script or go off-message (with consequences)
- The billionaires can be played against each other
- True ending: Win without either of them

---

### SCENARIO 8: "The Emergency"
**Difficulty:** Very Hard

#### Concept
Three days before the election, disaster strikes. Flood. Fire. Something. Suddenly the campaign doesn't matter—survival does.

A story about leadership when the stakes become real.

#### Key Elements
- Days 1-4 play normally
- Day 5: DISASTER EVENT changes everything
- Day 6-7: Crisis management replaces normal campaign
- Opponent becomes ally or rival in crisis response
- Winner determined by crisis handling, not campaign

---

### SCENARIO 9: "The Truth"
**Difficulty:** Very Hard

#### Concept
You have proof that your opponent committed a serious crime. Real proof. Undeniable. But releasing it will destroy their family, hurt innocent people, and make you look like the villain.

A story about whether winning is worth the cost.

#### Key Elements
- The evidence is introduced Day 1
- Every day, you choose: use it or don't
- Using it has escalating consequences
- NOT using it is also a strategy
- Multiple endings based on truth/silence balance

---

### SCENARIO 10: "The Final Campaign"
**Difficulty:** Brutal | **Unlocks after completing scenarios 1-9**

#### Concept
You've done this before. Many times. You've won and lost. Cheated and played fair. Built machines and torn them down.

Now it's the last race. The one that decides if any of it meant anything.

A story about legacy.

#### Key Elements
- References ALL previous campaign scenarios
- NPCs from previous campaigns appear
- Your "reputation" is aggregate of all previous choices
- Opponent is... complicated (twist reveal)
- True ending of the entire campaign mode
- Multiple endings based on HOW you played all 10 scenarios

#### The Twist (SPOILER)
Your opponent is yourself. A version of you that made different choices. The campaign is an internal reckoning with who you've become.

Final debate is against your own contradictions.

## DESIGN NOTES

### Why Fixed Scenarios?
1. **Learning tool:** Each scenario teaches a mechanic deeply
2. **Narrative satisfaction:** Procedural can't deliver story beats
3. **Streamer content:** Known scenarios = community knowledge = chat interaction
4. **Completion drive:** Gives collectors something to chase

### Balance with Quick Play
- Campaign teaches, Quick Play tests
- Campaign is finite, Quick Play is infinite
- Campaign has "canon" endings, Quick Play is your story
- Both feed into each other (unlocks, modifiers)

### Scenario Design Rules
1. Every scenario must teach ONE thing well
2. Opponent must be memorable and beatable fairly
3. At least one "signature moment" per scenario
4. Endings must feel earned (win or lose)
5. Callbacks to previous scenarios in later ones
